Job Opportunities

As of December 2023, the unemployment rate in Hamilton was 5.6%, a 0.3% increase over the previous month. The jobless rate was 7.6% lower than the June 2020 peak and lower than the long-term average. In comparison to the previous month, there were 700 fewer full-time employment in December 2023. In December, there were 1,500 fewer jobs overall due to the loss of 800 part-time roles. Although full-time employment was 2,700 jobs above the September 2023 low, it was still 1,400 jobs behind the peak in July 2023.
Healthcare: Hamilton's healthcare industry provides a range of employment options, such as jobs in clinics, hospitals, and research facilities.
Finance and Business Services: Hamilton, a major economic center, offers employment chances in banking, business services, and finance.

Education

For students, the average monthly cost of living in Hamilton is between CA$ 1,908 and CA$ 2,455. Hamilton may thus be a great option for overseas students looking for a low-cost, high-quality lifestyle.
Hamilton is proud of its robust 'Public school' system, which in Canada only refers to free education provided by the government.

Healthcare

The free healthcare system in Hamilton, Canada, is one of its best features. Hamilton is a hub for professional training and healthcare in the region. The City of Hamilton is offering free health services to you and your family, regardless of how long you have been in the country.
